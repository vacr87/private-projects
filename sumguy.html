An Honest Woman

This is about how she’s flooded
With the peace that issues from a tangled source.

Going about her day,
Always aware of the aperture.

She’s been told about Divine Peace,
But this woman is honest.

Going about her day …
Sometimes the aperture,
And her understanding looks
Backwards through the floodwaters, and
Into the aperture,
And into the tangled zone
Of peace only partly processed
To divine light.

That insane tangle
After divinity but before
Her going about her business,
And only she, this honest woman,
Sees how normal life
Is only not insane
Because it would be insane
To say everything is.

Commandment

I am monolithic, carved in stone,
I’m your thousand pound weight,
Your commandment, your measure.

You will never measure up to me.

I prevent the births
Of who would not suffer me.

I am monolithic.
You will never measure up.

I am love,
You will fail.

I am love,
I am a cross-eyed acrobat
Who I constantly prevent being born,
Which is the method I use to be
Humorless.

Don’t forget,
You have free will.

I command you name me,
You’ll probably name me
Death, but the failure I ensure
Is worse.

I am the crushing weight of your free will
On the wheel of failure and fiasco,
Collapse and insolvency, feel
My crushing weight,
Call me Commandment, the block,
The midnight sky of your home underground.

You don’t have problems, you are mine.
Nothing is heaver than I am.
Know me as
Your inability to ever be quite right.
Be rid of me, you cannot,

When things go wrong,
Your prayer to me
Has asked for it.

Get rid of me, you can’t.

When things go wrong
Your prayer to me
Has asked for it.

I am who I am, One and Only God,
And you’ll never measure up
To anything.

Exotic Bird (Again This Woman Finds Herself on Both Sides of Something)

Camouflaged desires
Rustling of sound,
She becomes attracted to the word,
Jaywalking,
Feeling the sound of the word camouflaged
Would have lacked the sharp, clipped sounds
To match her quick step.

Now on the other side of Reconciliation,
Acute knowledge that her movements
Orchestrate her emotions,
Her freewill becoming unable to resist
a vividness,
Long and smooth,
Beneath her surface aspirations,
Ambiguity screwing
Promiscuous hinges
Between the two sides of herself that
She believes in thoroughly,
A staging of emotion preventing her from realizing
That if she hadn’t jaywalked
She would have walked by the liquor store.

Folding a duplicate,
And she’s opened into existence
On opposite sides of the street at the same time.

On the opposite sides
Of anything
At the same time.

To get clear headed she must
Find a way to become real.

Of course she has Long
Accepted
She’s at all times
Suspended untouched
Between unpleasant fabrics,
A means to find herself when she’s in the dark.

She was in Peru once, or

Was the jungle in Borneo …?
But just applying makeup,
Eyebrow pencil, lipstick,
Knowing how to get to those
Tourist destinations of the desires,
At least.

She opens up her face, sees a girl.
Looking into the child’s innocent eyes
Her adult form sees that the child remembers her
From the future,
And even now, brushing the
Tangles out of her hair,
Cleaning strands from the bristles, she
Knows the day will arrive when her face will close.

This lets her see herself from all sides.
But she’s probably sideways
In a way sleeping would be most mortal, because
One view of her shows the bottoms of her feet,
The other the top of her head.
Again this woman finds herself on both sides of something.

Like someone has just come into the room,
Has seen the carved parrot,
Noticing the hinges,
And now she’s become somebody
Who doesn’t know which half,
But tolerates the thoughts of the person standing out there looking in
At her getting opinions
About how she should have handled the situation in the first place.

No,
I could have handled my part differently.
I see it now, it
Was never about him,
It was about me;

And he turns,
Decides not to go into the liquor store after all,
Turns for home,
The problem not ever about her, it was about me,

But she never found any rational way to talk about reconciliation,
Always the two of them,
On the opposite sides
Of anything
At the same time.

And they lived … and …

Love is Both of You Guessing Correctly at the Same Time

Mountain,
Stoic staircase
A flag for each complex
Of emotion,
Hoisted rags of
Correspondence in a language
Knowing not to ask
Where the mountain’s lover
Went after she died.

Mountain unrooted,
Untranslatable
Alphabet of the survivor’s square wing.
Stammering in a language
Of lost ground under his feet.

She of easy flight and easy arts,
Things that don’t make any sense,
But die and become absorbed
Into genius,
Unable to remember anything
Not making sense.

That’s all it ever was between the two,
Guessing correctly,
Dancing around the kitchen
Stirring onions, stemming cilantro,
But the music under them,
Adding a dash of this,
A dash of that,

And genius isn’t being smart,
But how to get clear enough
To let life guess you,
And that’s what happens to love,
Wouldn’t you say,
From experience?

Matriarchy

Grandmother’s kitchen, a light a certain worth,
A gamble you’d chosen that day
To wear yellow,
A wing with golden tomorrows.

Arranging a scrapbook providing alone-time
To daughters with their hopes.
Pandering a first light about great-great-grandmothers
Returning from someone’s memories to their kitchens
Pregnant with their parents.

I/thou, grandmother,
A yes to resonate with true seed,
You are never not pregnant
From the womb pre-dream,
Pregnant with your swimming great-great-grandmother,
Your great-great grandmother from whom you inwardly form,
Arriving out there
That beautiful afternoon with the faraway relatives
Of the hovering decency

Dressed in you, for bright vestment,
Dressed in who you are
Pre establishing in your great-great grandmother
The style you wear into her
First memory of herself.

Mother, Daughter

Mother and daughter,
Bathing,
Let you walk up,
Touch them —
Today one of the days
They exist as form.

You yourself may have enough sensitivity
To recognize the differences
Between the two feelings —
A certain feel when you are in your body,
And a different feel
When you exist as form.

When in your body
Autonomy, privacy, even sovereign
And defining authority within your Consciousness.

Then, when you are form
Privacy no longer an issue.
You are form,
You are a living and breathing idea,
You are an idea: to be shared.

Mother and daughter, so smooth
in their forms,
Ideas that want to be touched —
To share themselves.

Mother and daughter in their bodies
Bathing in utter and sovereign privacy,
Somewhere in the world sharing a towel,
Drying …
Maybe a leg now …
While you add to the

Totality of world experience running your Fingers over their forms
Changing inside yourself, the act of
Touching these forms teaching you
How to recognize the broader you,
Who contains you.

Here you are, running your hands over
Their forms, Mother, Daughter,
Recognizing and learning the differences
Of feel between when
You are in your body
And when you are form.

Touching them, you yourself are form,
And another part of your mind sees
How it feels to be in your body, seeing
Your reactions while interacting with these
Beautifully crafted
Mother and daughter forms.

And now, living both in your body
And outside your body as an idea
Sensitive to your body’s inner reactions,
You move through a brief experience
Of being yourself,
Yourself and complete, then the spell breaks,
You’re back in your body again,
Happy. Walking over to look at something else
That’s caught your interest.

Our First Possibility

I’m not hanging upside down, but you, friend, are down there with the damp-smelling earth and roots, head tipped back listening to the sound of my steps up here this spring morning taking a walk along the neighborhood of Sky-land I love most, with the sun aslant along the blue topsoil, strolling the easy path, delighting in how plasma ignites around my ankles tender, temporary bouquets that I kick into becoming stars like sea foam.

If I were to actually be somebody, then I’d be the person who gave the world it’s idea of wholeness, famous for giving the world the idea that one day back in the past something first began. When someone would have a feeling of one moment moving into the next, they’d thank me for giving them that idea letting them not see change not changing. I would invent wholeness, I am not upside down, but am who can both live in contentment, and at the same time drink the solar winds of infinity.

I am biology, you would be the early morning dream. You would live earlier than the idea of resolution. You would be where longing to have a lover and fear of a sky-lover would, by being the same world, keep you … too alive, exceedingly undecided …

All of this only someplace that somebody else will walk by. All of this but a waystation, a half-thought that lays a few feet off the path … and here comes somebody … without noticing us here … lost in the magnificence of her ability to be grounded in reality. And she’s not upside down.

And we’re fully aware that wide privacy is never not our first possibility.

Our Great and Delicate Inadequacy

The Sea is water,
The river runs with water.

Love flows love,
Fear flows love,
Hate flows love,
The sea is love,
The river is love,
Rain is love.

Here is love
There is love,
There is no
Now.

There is not tree,
There is no trying.

There is no river,
But there

IS
Love in the world.

There is no love
There is no love, there
Is no land for dying.

No land for living,
There are no rivers
Splashing any bottom rungs
Of ladders to the firmament,
Up where we see
Boats with blue hulls skimming
Trenches of hope,
Ruby dolphins frolicking truly tall liquid sunsets,
But only when some things are true

And others aren’t,
Never during times when nothing is not true,
When the endeavor called emoting
Lets it all happen at the bedside
Of our beloved
Who lays dying.

What remains … now?
With nothing of her remaining?

When the river is still here, flowing,
And remains and remains and remains
After the loss of color?

When the burn of fire is still here, and remains
After the loss of temperature.

When the river continues on, straight out,
And beyond the lip of the disassembling Waterfall of moments?

What remains of love,
A sound in the distance?
After your body is hollow if it,
After you are inside a tall mouth
That is a cave of wind-fire singing?

What remains then, of the song
After you’ve become the mouth of the singer?
What remains of death
Now that you live as that idea?

Once there is nothing prosaic in the world,
Once there is no such thing as profound,
What will you be creating then? For
By law of the person,
You cannot stop creating,

Even up from nothing to turn fire to religion,
Water to sacrament,
Your down at the heel
Left shoe into your bible.

How will you, please answer this question,
How will you stay here among your
Water glasses, and shoelaces, and gloves,
And pelicans and worms,
And Saturday nights out?

How will you ever again trust commonsense,
How will you keep from feeling nature
Is but your mother fantasy,
How will you feel
Alone after your beloved has died, what method after the night
You accompanied her departing soul
Too far out, too far out,
To, far out. To, far out,
When your single step from this world …
A half step … was a million miles long,
Coming up an inch short
Of completing that final stroll
The edge beyond which you have turned back Around, come back with your heart holding the Last untried way to love,
That is now your task to learn,
And … but now you ask,
Who WAS this woman?
Who?

And even you .. now you .. here…
Returning from reverie to your senses
Maybe fidgeting a finger,
The finger you’ve only now become conscious
Of moving?

But you’ve always know there exists
A way to love that nobody tires.

And this way, never before tired,
Has become your last remaining option ..
Now …
Speechless,
You haven’t met that part of yourself,
But can only believe there’s still a way
To love that you haven’t tried,
And the guide o lead you there …
A subject waiting to be created.
Today,
That’s all.
One thing at a time.

Querus Englemanii

Biologists call me,
Quercus engelmannii,
But to my neighbors
I’m someone to use, not talk about.

They have seen my limbs laying in the dirt for decades,
I do not rot, I could be stone, they plant me for fence posts.
They have seen fire sweep past,
If I burn, I could be stone, I burn slow,
They use me for firewood,
I’m a tribal member of harsh conditions.
I could be called,
Quercus Quiet as a Elder.
Coyote sleepy eyed in my inaudible shade,
Woodpecker’s granary of sunspots ,
Owl’s nest of ten thousand
Twiggy stars.

I don’t know your human word, eternal. Ten east winds pass while I breath once.
Nine animals with two legs, feet in boots
Creep by while I think twice.
A whole planet can come and go,
passing through a billion years,
Acorn parted from the twig at the start up of time,
Still … falling.

Everything held in the air … by something …
Gravity,
Our friend and our foil gravity
Pays homage to bottomless space…
And in this …
For you to hold …
The acorn midair,
Falling through
An eternal emptiness
Before you have feelings in you again,
And this is the way we live,
Water drops holding without falling,
Pearls holding without falling,
A return, cycling windows of wholeness
With nothing we know about, blessedly, happening,
When you love
Holding without falling,
Born into this.

Reconfiguration

A man knows a woman ten thousand ways.
He who knows her five thousand at the same time
Properly has released all she is not.

The released five thousand ways of knowing her
Had been all the man knew beyond doubt.

Joining what he knows about the approaching days
After his life has closed,
Same as the day before it had first opened,
And he takes love and bequeaths the bird
The wind from the mountaintop.

Sensation
Takes one apart,
Out of one’s previous configuration,
Lost rapture,
Reconfigurations, the beautiful
Sequencing of self,
Flow of reconfiguring …
But later … back a man again,
Unable to find the language,
And she too had reached
The home she couldn’t return with.

Sprout for Ethics

Ethics, seeds to eventually sprout behavior,
Planted down into the rich,
Hyper lush soil
Of our choices.

But this is a cycle,
Field woken to the light of the moon
By asking for the plow.
Lunation in ethics knowing when to swell
When to bleed across our bright fruit,
The apple no impersonality
But joy in the ten arms of that
Procreative tangle.
And maybe the apple
Well schooled in the birth and death of goodness.

The apple?

Yes, because to poetry we must blend
The attitude of simply taking things as they are,
Which requires something to appear,
The woman pushing her brightly painted
Produce cart,
Fruits, vegetables, mushrooms, sprouts,
Ground and tree nuts,
And herbs … hitting a rut, something rolled off, but
She didn’t see it happen, and we’re naming it the apple.

Look at her, with that red, red scarf,
Look how she’s danced away from her produce cart
Dances the fandango, no
Wonder she didn’t see the apple roll off
… what? An apple? Something has rolled off
This happy soul’s brightly painted cart.
Can we call it a red red apple?

Maybe we should shy away from naming the apple.

Why should we?

Because the red, red, red, red apple, wouldn’t you believe,
Might from the beginning make obedience be
Life’s guiding light,
Wouldn’t you believe, so to speak?

Oh yea! I see what you’re saying.
If we name it the apple
We’ll prejudice ourselves by the fruits
Of that certain act of disobedience
Befalling the famous garden
Whose name we won’t mention? But
I can’t think of a single fruit
That has not sprung from passion.

Yes, it is as you say, we’re in agreement about everything.
We can’t give up passion.
We can’t.
Passion will never guarantee
100 % that well survive it,
But it’s not really risk
To throw yourself to passion,
Life is beyond risk.

And agreeing there is no risk in life
Is all it takes
For ethics to sprout up into behavior
From the core of hyper lush passion,
And spread out the most amazing, hyper lush,
Dance floor.
Hand me another drugged flower,
They’re delicious.

Right on, my sister, I’ll dance to that, but
How do we tell good passion from bad?

Watch me … the kind that makes us dance the fandango!

Swan

Moon rising to a
Red night bird
Woman changing slowly
Into a chair,
Resting in the seat
Of her emotions.
What hovers such honied air?

A swan chair
In the light
Of that swan moon
On the shores of that
Silvered lake.

Seat of emotions, a
Little boat,
Because it was a swan,
Moonlit in the elegant,
Multi-honied air
That aquifer,
That
Moonlit night.

Swan would be a stork,
The chair lifting
A leg,
The bird’s

Red mouthed lover
With a leg of flesh
Dangling a hot red slipper,
Her gown of bleeding white.

Up from the seat
Of the passions
Seated in such changes,
You had just cut

Your finger …
You’re bleeding …

On a sharp kitchen knife.

And you went back to
Your red bird.
You are that moonlit night.
You are that swan moon.

Someone held
You aloft in the swan
Moonlight
That windy night, you
Bled out, bled
The needs of your love,
Creating the skies over Kansas, now, someone had
Thrown you to the wind, red bird,
Blown from some mountain in Colorado,
Blown from the hands
Of someone you loved.

The wind … wind …

The wind,
Would they release you,
Would they release you,
Crimson bird,
Scarlet scrap in the wind?
Would winged passion
Be bound?

Let the winds,
Let the winds tear your love to paper scraps,

Let the winds blow you apart

So your passions
Will fly to that part of your meaning
Exceeding who
You will ever be.

Cardinal bird,
Red bird,
Scrap of love,
Over western Kansas
By now.

The empty,

Bleeding prison,
Servitude in the ore
Mines darkened
In the
Cloud Mountain.

Free them, free your passions, let your
Passions fly away,
Your passions will soon
Come back home.
They will! They’ll come back to you!

The seat of your passion
Wings the air,
Has wings!
A woman helpless in her seat of passion, winging
…. The wind dying from around her
Airborne chair,
Drifting …
Falling …
… maybe bouncing a last gust, now a

Plastic bag
Stuck in a
Last chance
Prophet’s
Burning bush.

Oh Bleeding Swan, come to me,
I’ll tend your wound, dear Swan,
Swan, your finger
Bleeding, staining my hands,
Stained white

The Invisible Flower

Easily but half open half closed,
Her attention a basin for water.

An uplifting floated on the surface,
Her thin blouse with golden shoulders.

Treasury of Burgundy,
Silk wine.

Why would a women go further?
Where she doesn’t feel her clothes.

Easily but half open half unbuttoned,
His attention an egret
At her shore.

The most gentle riot,
Breeze stitched with silver.
He is a tall and broad mahogany.

Between the pool
And the mahogany
A table, a vase,
The invisible flower that sprouts
Wherever two gather.

Easily she wonders
If he has asked the future of his heart
Into breath.

So easy, easily
Each daubs upon the vase
Touches of adoration,

The invisible flower
Hovering the air
Above the vase,
Between
Every couple,
Savage for survival.

The hidden flower,
Wherein couples meet
Savage for survival,
To tenderness,
Warmth riotously
Blooming.
Warmth
Riotously blooming.

Touch

God is the tip of a living finger … that is to say,
Things are themselves while we’re
Conscious of sensation,
Touching.

We would not say we had ten gods,
The freewill of skin,
To itself an articulate chamois
Even when drug by sight,
Vicarious touch
A dove
Resting on a jumpy thigh of Jupiter.

The surfaced world,
Surfaces broken to newly
Existent surfaces, surfaces,
And touch.

Even if the window glass doesn’t have to
Realize it’s smooth,
Or the weathered wood it’s rough,
We teach those dumb forms
The reality of being,
And yes, after
Becoming so superficially educated,
They say yes and become everything.

What is the delight
Of the imaginary?
At what instances is it entirely possible
For you to slide,
Hot bare feet over glass, or
Slip your leg
Down into the rains that have weathered wood?

This is me speaking — who
Am a surface — feel me. feel me! I am all that is!

Fingers soar, bank left, dive, loop, climb to the sun,
They have joined the doves!
With your touch upon me you’re creating me …
Flying free
On the ability to be the god
Of what you want,
And we your subjects, we each of surface,
Delight at your touch rising up into
And falling from being.
Delight at your touch rising and falling from being, mutually
Delighting in our own truest ongoing creation — you have been created,
The creation ongoing,
Newly.
Feeling it, your finger’s eternal lessons
Without moving … but slide them, feeling, pressing,
Sensation creating everything.

You are touching.
Affective …
Don’t waste a moment more!

Pull her body close, hold him to yourself ..
Life! True Person …
True Person …
… touch all the way through!

Wing of Color

That long, beautiful, feathered thing
Feathered out from east to west
Through rain unwet, up
In clouds not opaque —
That wing of color up there!
You must remember
That long unwet, not opaque
Wing of color that day,
When you took that long walk with yourself …
Tipped your head back,
Up to that long beautiful wing
All alone up there …

That single wing of color
Like you’d see
Most days …
You must remember
That long unwet, not opaque
Colored wing up there in the sky all alone,
But for the first time,
Stopped from your walk, looking up,
The display of a surprising
Mating wing
— how unexpected! Wings in pairs! —
The wing of understanding
From south to north that
Not unlucky day,
The Engraved Bird of Passing Time
Displaying two shoulders,
Meridians of the heavens
On moving time,
The wing of color crossing under
The wing of understanding
Passing in front of
The empty hole that
Before that moment
Had been your heart.

Your heart become a rain of rainbows
Curved over the dome of the
Not opaque sky that rainy unwet day
From the horizon all around,

Directly Under the center of the umbrella sky,
The intersection of rainbows that umbrella day,
Bows covering over the hole in your heart.
The intersection of
The Winged Rainbow of Color, and
The Winged Rainbow of Understanding
Looking directly down
At your place on the top of the little planet
Dancing with your
Wobbling sky umbrella.

The Engraved Bird of Passing Time
A sky with Rainbows for wings,
Umbrella struts for wingtips on the rim
Around the bowl of planet earth,
The cupped brown nest
While engraving silver
— Introspection in this case becoming metallurgy —
Flutters us home
To our cavorting mother
Of pearl moment we never knew we lived through
In the first place.

Inlaid abalone birds, they could be.
Room divider of our aesthetics, they could be.
Impossible, inlaid, lucent,
Curling up out of that place without thickness,
And you forgot your house,
Your disregard for your household
Let it fall down flat all around you
When you were
Standing there ready to dance,
Flat on wings that have become a carpet,
And there could never not exist the spinning dancer,
Spinning off drops of nectar. And you are free color
color of understanding … free … so …
then tell us, please,
how nectar beads on the flesh
of what you have scoured for signs
of redemption.

Keep to yourself, please, what you think of
coming up empty.

Shall we gather around the campfire,
raise wolf pelts and crack spheres of glass for the sun?
Throw in more of what if burnt would from wind incinerate the howl?

Tell us — how does nectar appear?
What nail hole waits
with its gift of purity?

From the palm of what woman’s hand,
what will seep up out of a pore in her skin,
to glisten in the understanding
the artifact, a fragment
from the idea that each thing
reclining in itself
will remain itself,
is itself,
cold, white stone, hard, pure,
that would,
on a new light of her soul be,
the burn of passion.
